My daughter was give an older iPod touch fourth-generation with 6.4 Gb of storage. This is great little device since it has a built in camera, she can listen to music, record audio, and watch videos. She loves to run around just capturing what she sees. We had a trip coming up that would involve being on a plane for 4 hours and I wanted to put some of her favorite shows on her iPod to watch on the plane while traveling.

In an effort to maximize the amount of videos that I could fit on the device I turned to using FFmpeg to reduce the mp4 video files down to a resolution that was watchable on the iPod. With a quick google search I found that I could use the scale command to set a smaller video scale. My original video was a 720x480 pixel mp4 file. I could reduce those video down to a smaller 480x360 or 240x180 video size to help with saving space. Below is a list of those FFmpeg scale commands. 

# 720p
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -s 720x480 -c:a copy output.mp4
# 480p
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -s 480x360 -c:a copy ouput.mp4
# 240p
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -s 240x180 -c:a copy ouput.mp4

I watched both the 480x360 and 240x180 resolutions on the iPod. I have to say the 480x360 is much better to watch but the 240x180 is watchable. My 5 year old daughter didn't seem to care between the two so I went with the 240x180 resolution. I compared the final file sizes between the three videos and found with the 240x180 resolution I could reduce the storage needed by 43%. Again, not a good video quality but now I could now get roughly 7.31 hrs of video per 1 GB of storage. This is a lot of value to my daughter per GB of storage.
